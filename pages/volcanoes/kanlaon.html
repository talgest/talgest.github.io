A stratovolcano in Negros Province

Kanlaon Volcano

weekly reports

SUMMARY

Kanlaon volcano, also spelled Canlaon, is the most active volcanoe of the central Philippines, forms the highest point on the island of Negros. The massive 2435-m high stratovolcano is dotted with fissure-controlled pyroclastic cones and craters, many of which are filled by lakes. The summit of Canlaon contains a broad elongated northern caldera with a crater lake and a smaller, but higher, historically active crater to the south.

The largest debris avalanche known in the Philippines traveled 33km to the SW from Canlaon. Historical eruptions, recorded since 1866, have typically consisted of phreatic explosions of small-to-moderate size that produce minor ashfalls near the volcano.

HISTORY

1986 - 1989 Eruptions
As emissions happened at Kanlaon volcano in June 1986. Last June 21, 1986 ash reached a height of 4000m above the summit combined with an earthquake over 8km from the summit. A loud sound by the hazard accompanied the ash ejection. The eruption of Kanlaon Volcano in April 1987 was preceded by an increase in Sulphate concentration at Mambukal 9.75 km N-NW of Kanlaon's summit A phreatic eruption occurred at Bulusan Volcano on November 27, 1994, which ejected an ash plume at 1.5km altitude.

A second ash explosion in December 3 deposited ashes 7km from the volcano. A strong phreatic explosion on December 12, 1994, created a 3km high ash cloud and caused ashfall 16 km from the volcano. An eruption in January 21, 1995, was accompanied by rumbling noises, lightning flashes over the summit, and light ashfall at the San Benon Observatory 5km SW. Ash emissions happened in June 1988. Traces of ash fell in the village of Mananawin on the SE slope at 980m elevation. On June 27, 1988 ash emissions reached a height of 500m above the crater, and ash fell on the SW flank. Ash emissions happened at Kanlaon volcano between October and December 1989. On November 3, 1989, an eruption reached a height of 1200 m above the crater. Ground inflation occurred a few hours before ash emissions.

1992 - 1996 Eruption
A minor earthquake, ash emission, and ashfall occurred at Kanlaon volcano on June 10, 1992. Small phreatic explosions happened at Kanlaon volcano on August 25, 1993, and September 3, 1993. An explosion at Kanlaon volcano killed three people in August 10, 1996. There were two climbing groups at the summit at the moment of the explosion. Eighteen people were hospitalized due to injuries caused mainly by falling ejecta.

2002 - 2006 Eruptions
An increase in seismicity during January 2002 was followed by a small ash emission in November 28, 2002. On March 17, 2003, a volcanic plume was observed at Kanlaon volcano. Small eruptions of ash and steam reaching about 100m above the active crater were observed in the first week of June. In June 8, 2003, an ashfall occurred at Canlaon City. After July 23, 2003, weak steam emission was noticed, and seismic activity returned to low levels.

A short amount of ash emission began at Kanlaon volcano in January 21, 2005. The emissions reached 500m above the crater. The eruption was phreatic. On March 20, 2005, ashfall was reported in La Carlota City of Negros Occidental in 5 km west of the volcano. From March 24, 2005, to April 4, 2005, occasional ash emissions reached 1 km above the volcano. Ashfall was reported in different towns. Eruptions began in June 3, 2006, and 23 ash eruptions happened from July 25, 2006. The eruptions were phreatic and emitted ash and steam around 2km above the crater. No significant seismic activity occurred before or after the ash emissions, indicating the explosions were near surface hydrothermal events.

2015 Eruptions
Ash emissions happened at Kanlaon volcano in December 2015. A 4 km radius permanent exclusion zone exists around the volcano.
