Bulusan Volcano

weekly reports

SUMMARY

Bulusan is located in the most-Southern part of Luzon. It was constructed along the rim of the 11 diameter km Irosin caldera, which was formed about 36,000 years ago. It lies at the SE end of the Bicol volcanic arc. A broad, flat moat is located below the topographically prominent SW rim of the Irosin caldera; the NE rim is buried by the andesitic complex.

Bulusan is bounded by several other large intracaldera lava domes and cones, including the prominent Mount Jormajan lava dome on the SW flank. The summit is desolated and contains a 300-m-wide, 50-m-deep crater. Three small craters are located on the SE flank. Many moderate explosive eruptions have been recorded since the mid-19th century.

HISTORY

1983 Eruptions
Small phreatic eruptions occurred at the summit crater on June 25, 1983, and June 29, 1983. Ashes reached to a height of 1.2km.

1988 Eruptions
In February 20, 1988, a small ash eruption occurred at Bulusan Volcano. The eruption was accompanied by lightning at the summit. in February 24, 1988, a magnitude 6.9 earthquake occurred 80km NE of Bulusan. The earthquake possibly caused a landslide which was observed on the volcano's middle to lower flank.

1994-95 Eruptions
A phreatic eruption happened at Bulusan Volcano on November 27, 1994, which released an ash plume at 1.5km altitude. A second ash explosion on December 3 deposited ashes 7km from the volcano. A strong phreatic explosion on December 12, 1994, created a 3km high ash cloud and caused ashfall 16 km from the volcano. An eruption on January 21, 1995, was accompanied by rumbling noises, lightning flashes over the summit, and light ashfall at the San Benon Observatory 5km SW.

2006 - 2007 Eruptions
A summit eruption occurred at Bulusan Volcano last March 21, 2006, after being silent for 11 years. Researchers believe that there were ten explosions up until June 28, 2006. The eruptions in 2006 were the result of small volumes of magma with an overlying water-saturated zone beneath the summit. Explosive eruptions and ashfall happened between October 10, 2006, and May 12, 2007. On July 31, 2007, an explosion ejected ash to an altitude of 6.6 km.
