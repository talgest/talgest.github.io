Taal Volcano

weekly reports

SUMMARY

Taal is one of the most active volcanoes in the Philippines and has made some of its most devastating historical eruptions. Though not topographically prominent, its prehistorical eruptions have greatly changed the landscape of SW Luzon. The 15 x 20 km Talisay (Taal) caldera is largely filled by Lake Taal, whose 267 km2 surface lies only 3 m above sea level.

The maximum depth of the lake is 160m, and some eruptive centers lie submerged beneath the lake. The 5km-wide Volcano Island in north-central Lake Taal is the location of all observed eruptions. The island is consist of coalescing small stratovolcanoes, tuff rings, and scoria cones. Intense pyroclastic flows and surges have caused many fatalities.

HISTORY

1911 Eruption
The eruption of the Taal volcano in 1911 destroyed most life on the island and caused damage to the area that is surrounding the volcano. 1335 people were killed by the eruption.

1965 - 1977 Eruption
A phreatomagmatic eruption of Taal volcano between September 28, 1965, to September 30, 1965. The eruption enveloped an area of approximately 60 square kilometers with ash more than 25cm thick. The 1965 eruption was the first since 1749 to be located on the flanks of the island. The eruption caused a base surge which caused loss of life and damage from the created tsunami. The base surge was initiated by water entering the magma chamber. Ash clouds rose 20km high during the eruption and ash fell 80km away. Lapilli, a rock fragment from a volcano fell 9km downwind from the explosion crater. Ash and steam erupted on November 9, 1977. Ash and steam erupted on 9 November 1977.

2010 - 2012 Unrest
On April 26, 2010, volcanic earthquakes increased. The composition of Main Crater Lake water has shown the above-normal values of Magnesium Chloride, Sulfate Chloride, and Total Dissolved Solids. There has been ground steaming accompanied by hissing sounds on the northern and northeast sides of the main crater. PHIVOLCS has raised the status of the volcano to Alert Level 2, which indicates the volcano is undergoing magmatic intrusion which may lead to an eruption. The main crater has been marked as off-limits due to the risk of an eruption. This Unrest state lasted until 2012 as there was a large increase in seismic activity at Taal Volcano on November 22, 2012, to November 23, 2012.

2020 Eruption
A huge eruption began at Taal volcano, on January 12, 2020. Ash emissions reached 55,000ft altitude. Reports of ash falling at Alabang, 45km north of the volcano and Metro Manila. Flights were canceled at Ninoy Aquino International Airport, Manilla due to ash. Taal volcano progressed to a magmatic eruption on January 13, 2020. On the evening of January 13, 2020, 500m high lava fountains occurred at the volcano.
