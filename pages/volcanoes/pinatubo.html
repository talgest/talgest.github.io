Mount Pinatubo

weekly reports

SUMMARY

Mount Pinatubo, a volcano in western Luzon, Philippines that erupted in 1991 for the first time in 600 years and caused widespread devastation. Mount Pinatubo is located in the provinces of Zambales and rose to a height of about 4,800 feet prior to its eruption. Before 1991 Pinatubo had a height of 1745 m, and was only 200m higher than nearby mountains which obscured it from view. The surrounding mountains are remains of an older Mt Pinatubo.

The largest eruption at Mt Pinatubo volcano happened 35,000 years ago, and produced pyroclastic flow deposits up to 100m thick on all sides of the volcano. The Sacobia eruption period 17,000 years ago produced two debris flow deposits which are visible on the north bank of the Sacobia River.

HISTORY

Eruptions of Mount Pinatubo
The largest eruption at Mt Pinatubo volcano occurred 35,000 years ago, and produced pyroclastic flow deposits up to 100 m thick on all sides of the volcano. The Sacobia eruption period 17,000 years ago produced two debris flow deposits which are visible on the north bank of the Sacobia River.


The 1991 Eruptions

June 7, 1991
An ash eruption sent a cloud 8km high. Alert level 4 was issued stating an eruption could happen within 24 hours. The danger zone extended to a 20km of radius. Lava reached the surface and formed a small dome at 1km northwest of the main crater.

A short amount of ash emission began at Kanlaon volcano in January 21, 2005. The emissions reached 500m above the crater. The eruption was phreatic. On March 20, 2005, ashfall was reported in La Carlota City of Negros Occidental in 5 km west of the volcano. From March 24, 2005, to April 4, 2005, occasional ash emissions reached 1 km above the volcano. Ashfall was reported in different towns. Eruptions began in June 3, 2006, and 23 ash eruptions happened from July 25, 2006. The eruptions were phreatic and emitted ash and steam around 2km above the crater. No significant seismic activity occurred before or after the ash emissions, indicating the explosions were near surface hydrothermal events.

9th June 1991
Sulfur dioxide began escaping again from the volcano. A large eruption of ash. People evacuated from a 20 km radius. 10th June 1991 Evacuations began at Clark Air Base around 14,400 people.

June 12, 1991
Ash eruptions to 20km above the volcano on Philippine Independence Day.

June 13, 1991
Ash eruption to 25km high followed by 28 hours without an explosion. Earthquakes continued.

June 14, 1991
An intense explosion at 1:09 pm. Eruption column to 21km in 15 minutes. Nuee ardentes devastated the main valleys radiating from Mt Pinatubo.

June 15, 1991
Typhoon Yunya passed 100km northeast of Pinatubo. Mudflows start pouring down the mountain at 30km/hr. At 3:39 pm the main eruption began. In 9 hours, 90% of the material for the eruption was released from the volcano. Cauliflower columns of ash, gas, and steam rose 34km high and traveled 400km. At 4:30 pm the summit of Pinatubo began to sink. The evacuation area was increased to a 40km radius. The eruption stopped at 10:34 pm.
