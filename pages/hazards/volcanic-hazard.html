LATERAL BLAST

A lateral blast is a volcanic eruption that is directed laterally or from the side of a volcano rather than upwards from the summit. It is caused by the outward expansion of flanks due to the rising of magma on a volcano. When this hazard happens it makes the magma flow outward easily.

ASHFALLS

Volcanic ashfalls is the removal of ash from the air from eruption plumes released during explosive volcanic eruptions. Small pointed pieces of rocks, minerals, and volcanic glass with the size of sand. Erupted by a volcano is called volcanic ash. Volcanic ash is formed during explosive volcanic eruptions.
